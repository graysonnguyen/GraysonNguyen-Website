If photography and I were married, we would have just hit our 9-year anniversary this October. Photography has gifted me the ability to capture the world through my eyes. Creativity has always been important in my life which is evident through my photography. Some days it may be 14.2 miles for photographs atop a dome, others it is driving for at least an hour before finding a location beautiful enough to complement an equally beautiful subject, and sometimes it is a 3 second walk outside to freeze dew on leaves.

To me, photography is more than just saving the moment; it is about telling a story from my perspective. Photography allowed me to capture priceless moments and experience much more that taking the photograph it self. Wading into rivers, twisting an ankle and pushing on further, and numerous scratches on arms and legs just for that perfect photograph. When revisiting old photographs, I am instantly taken over as snippets of that day poor into my head: the Merced River in Yosemite, the Narrows in Zion, and Old-Faithful in Yellowstone. Photographs have reminded me of much more than vacations and have helped me rediscover a 90th birthday party for my grandma, a Christmas Eve full of games, or a day spent with a best friend that could never be recreated.

At the start of my junior year I landed an internship with a professional wedding photographer. I finally did it. I finally proved to myself that I was good enough. I had been taking photographs for my enjoyment and soon appreciated the joy of taking them for others. Seeing the happiness of countless brides and grooms as I helped immortalize their very special day has deepened my love for photography and made me realize much more. My love of photography has shed light on a part of me that wants to help others and pushed me towards a career in a field of that nature.
